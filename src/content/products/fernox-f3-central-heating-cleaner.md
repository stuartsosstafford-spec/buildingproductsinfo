## In-Depth Look

Central heating systems accumulate contamination over time. Iron oxide sludge from corroding radiators, limescale from hard water areas, and flux residue from the original installation all build up inside the pipework and components. The symptoms are familiar to any plumber — radiators with cold spots at the bottom, noisy boilers, pump failures and reduced efficiency. Fernox F3 is the first step in dealing with this.

### The Clean-Then-Protect Process

The standard maintenance process for central heating systems follows a two-stage approach: clean with F3, then protect with F1. F3 is added to the system water and the heating is run normally for at least one hour, allowing the cleaner to circulate and loosen deposits. For systems with heavy contamination, it can be left circulating for up to seven days. The system is then drained and flushed — ideally power flushed — to remove the loosened contaminants. Once the flush water runs clear, the system is refilled and F1 Protector is added to prevent future contamination.

### Power Flushing Companion

F3 is at its most effective when used as part of a power flush. The power flushing machine circulates water at high velocity through the system while F3 loosens the deposits, and individual radiators are isolated and flushed in turn. The combination of chemical cleaning and mechanical flushing produces the best results and is the recommended approach for systems that have never been treated or have been neglected for years.

### Verdict

Fernox F3 is the essential first step in central heating system maintenance. It is not a standalone solution — it must be followed by thorough flushing and the addition of F1 Protector — but without it, cleaning a contaminated system effectively is significantly harder. A standard product that every plumber should carry alongside F1.
