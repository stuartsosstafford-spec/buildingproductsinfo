## In-Depth Look

OSB (Oriented Strand Board) has largely replaced plywood as the go-to structural sheet material on UK building sites, and OSB3 is the grade most commonly specified. The "3" designation indicates it is suitable for load-bearing applications in humid conditions, which covers the vast majority of construction uses.

### Structural Properties

The manufacturing process bonds large wood strands together under heat and pressure, with strands oriented in specific directions to maximise strength. This gives OSB3 predictable and reliable performance characteristics that are consistent from sheet to sheet — an advantage over natural timber products where grain variation can create weak spots. At 11mm thickness, this sheet is appropriate for roof decking, wall sheathing, and racking, though 18mm is generally preferred for flooring applications where greater load-bearing capacity is needed.

### Moisture Resistance

The OSB3 classification means the board uses moisture-resistant resin binders that allow it to perform in humid environments without structural degradation. However, it is important to understand the limits — OSB3 is not waterproof. Prolonged direct exposure to standing water will cause the board to swell, particularly at cut edges. In practice, this means it is perfectly suitable for roof decking under felt or membrane, wall sheathing behind cladding, and internal flooring, but it should not be left permanently exposed to the weather without protection.

### Verdict

OSB3 Sterling Board at 11mm is a reliable, cost-effective structural sheet material that serves well in the applications it is designed for. It handles roofing, sheathing, and general construction duties with predictable performance, and its lower cost compared to plywood makes it the practical choice for most structural sheet requirements.
