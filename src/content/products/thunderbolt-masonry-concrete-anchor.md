## In-Depth Look

The shift from traditional expansion anchors to screw-in concrete fixings has been one of the quiet revolutions in construction fixings over the past decade. Thunderbolt anchors are among the most widely used examples of this type, offering a compelling combination of speed, strength, and simplicity.

### Speed of Installation

The key advantage is the single-step process. Drill a hole with a 6mm SDS bit, clear the dust, and drive the Thunderbolt in with a socket or impact driver. There is no separate plug to insert, no need to push the fixing through the item being attached, and no expansion force to manage during tightening. On a job that requires dozens or hundreds of fixings, such as mounting cable tray or securing steelwork, this adds up to significant time savings.

### Load Performance

The M8 x 75mm size provides strong pull-out and shear resistance in good quality concrete, making it suitable for a wide range of medium to heavy-duty applications. Performance is dependent on the quality of the substrate; in well-cured concrete with good aggregate, the fixing performs reliably. In lightweight block or poor quality masonry, pull-out values drop and an alternative fixing strategy may be needed.

### Verdict

Thunderbolt concrete anchors are a practical, time-saving fixing for anyone working regularly with concrete and solid masonry. The M8 x 75mm size strikes a good balance between load capacity and ease of installation, covering the majority of bracket and steelwork fixings on site. The main requirement is accurate drilling and clean holes, so investing in quality SDS bits is advisable when using these fixings in volume.
